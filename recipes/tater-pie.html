Roasted Roots Recipe

I've loved to read my entire life. Growing up, I devoured multiple books a week. If I was inside, I was probably on the couch or in bed with a book. If I was outside, I was probably sitting on the swing with a book. Going to libraries or bookstores was my idea of the perfect outing. I hoarded books and had numerous favourite authors and series. In elementary school, the Redwall series was at the top of that list.

The Redwall books are a beautifully descriptive book series full of anthropomorphic woodland creatures. They fight, love, display compassion and bravery...and most of all, they eat. And oh, how they eat! Delicious feasts and humble meals, decadent desserts and rich road food, drinks, candies, salads, soups...All are described in exquisite detail.

As a child I was thrilled to discover an official cookbook for the Redwall series. I've cooked from it often in my life, but my favourite recipes have been memorized, tweaked, and perfected as I've grown up. Here's one of the best from the lot - known in the books as Mole's Favourite, Deeper'n'Ever, Turnip'n'Tater'n'Beetroot Pie. It's a hearty, filling meal full of root vegetables, and is perfect for a chilly autumn evening.

You will need...

  • Potatoes (any will do, but Yukon Gold are my favourite for this)
  • Carrots
  • Turnip
  • Beetroot
  • Rutabaga
  • Garlic cloves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Rosemary and thyme - fresh, if you've got 'em!
  • Olive oil

Cooking time!

  1. Peel the vegetables if that's your preference, or leave the skin on for more flavour.
  2. Mince the garlic, and dice all the other veggies.
  3. Heat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  4. Use olive oil to grease a glass pan.
  5. Pour vegetables into the pan. Add some olive oil, salt and pepper, and your herbs.
  6. Cover the pan with foil. Bake for 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until all vegetables are soft.

This meal is fantastic and naturally vegan, but if you'd like you can also add cheese or ground beef (or both!). Both are fantastic additions.

Happy eating!
