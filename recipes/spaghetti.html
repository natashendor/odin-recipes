Spaghetti Recipe

Growing up, spaghetti was always a treat at my house. My mom always made it with Italian sausage, and would usually serve corn or garlic bread (or both!) on the side. We'd wrap the noodles around our forks and dig in on special occasions.

My mom's recipe, while delicious, was also pretty basic. Italian sausage cooked with minced garlic, jarred spaghetti sauce, and noodles. I still love it as a simple go-to meal, but I've become enamored of how easy it is to spruce it up with just a few ingredients.

I have Celiac disease, so I cook this with rice noodles. My husband actually prefers them to regular noodles - they're a little less heavy, and have a milder taste. However, use whatever kind of noodles you prefer!

From my table to yours, here's the tastiest way to cook spaghetti that I've found. I hope it brings you joy!

You will need...

  • Rice noodles
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage
  • Garlic cloves (I use an entire head)
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 1-2 cups mushrooms, to taste
  • 1 jar marinara or spaghetti sauce (I like Kirkland's marinara sauce)
  • Olive oil
  • Basil (fresh is preferred, but dried works just as well)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Cooking time!

  1. Pour water in a large pot and bring to a boil. Add noodles. Cook according to instructions, and drain when done.
  2. While the noodles are cooking, mince your garlic finely. Dice the onion, mushrooms, and bell pepper.
  3. Pour olive oil into a pan. Heat over medium heat.
  4. When oil is hot, add garlic and onions. Cook until aromatic, or until the onions begin to turn translucent.
  5. Add your meat and diced bell pepper. Break up with a stirring spoon as needed.
  6. When the meat is mostly cooked, add mushrooms. Stir periodically until fully cooked.
  7. Reduce heat to low. Add jarred sauce, salt, pepper, and basil. Stir frequently until heated through.
  8. Scoop sauce over noodles and enjoy!

Happy eating!
