Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies

My grandfather was a professional candy-maker. He traveled the fair circuit before I was born, making and selling fudge, divinity, and these cookies. By the time I was born he had long since retired - but in his workshop he kept the big copper pot, the marble slab for rolling the fudge, and all of his tools. From time to time the extended family would get together and whip up a few batches of fudge the old-fashioned way - making forty to eighty pounds in one day. It would then be distributed as gifts.

Of course, making such large batches wasn't exactly convenient for day to day treats. After much trial and tribulation, we were able to come up with a stovetop variant of the peanut butter oatmeal cookies. They were a holiday treat when I was a kid; we always left these specific cookies out for Santa on Christmas Eve.

It's a deceptively easy recipe! Simple ingredients make whipping up a batch a snap...but the temperature and timing are very important to make sure the cookies set up right.

You will need...

  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 3 cups oatmeal
  • One dash vanilla

Cooking time!

  1. Lay out parchment paper in preparation for the cookies.
  2. Melt the butter in a large pot on the stove.
  3. Add sugar and milk. Stir.
  4. Heat the mixture until boiling.
  5. Stir continuously at a high boil for 1 minute.
  6. Turn off heat and add peanut butter. Stir until combined.
  7. Add oatmeal. Stir quickly - the cookies will begin to set.
  8. Scoop cookie-sized dollops of the mixture onto the parchment paper.
  9. Lick the spoon!
  10. Allow to cool - they'll set as their temperature reduces.
  11. Enjoy - and leave one out for Santa!

Happy eating!
